WORKING AGREEMENT
-----------------
New, durable, cross-repo standard — not scoped to this repo alone:

- **Evidence Discipline is unconditional**, added to every `AGENTS.md`
  (workspace root, `architecture-rosetta-stone`, `vs-mcp-bridge`,
  `BlogAI`): every claim about system or code behavior carries a basis
  — `authoritative`, `primary-source`, `claude-reasoning`, or
  `unverified` — using the exact vocabulary already established in
  `Standards/source-of-truth.md`. This does **not** require a trigger
  phrase to hold; it is the default, precisely because Bill does not
  always prompt with perfect precision and the safety needs to survive
  that.
- A new named skill, `investigate` (added to all 4 skill locations and
  all 4 `ai-skills` backup namespaces), is the *structured, heavier*
  version of the same rule — for formal multi-subsystem root-cause
  triage or any stakeholder-facing causation/status document. It scopes
  subsystems explicitly (including marking black boxes as such), labels
  every claim as it's made, refuses to let an unlabeled claim become
  load-bearing for later steps, and requires the full causal chain to be
  verified before any root-cause or defect-classification language is
  used.
- `vs-mcp-bridge`'s own `AGENTS.md` carries an explicit
  "Regulated-Industry Default" section: treat the least common
  denominator for this product's users as a regulated-industry client
  now, during beta, not as a later special case — grounded directly in
  the fact that this includes Bill's own employer within a few years.

METHOD DISCOVERIES
--------------------
- A safety discipline that only activates on a trigger phrase fails
  exactly when it's needed most — under pressure, with an incomplete or
  leading prompt, before anyone remembers to say the magic words. Bill's
  own incident happened *before* he thought to ask the corrective
  question; the fix isn't a better magic phrase, it's not needing one.
- Writing a new skill is exactly the moment its own stated principles
  are easiest to violate by accident — the first draft here embedded
  product-specific content into what was supposed to be a generic
  procedure. Catching that before propagating, rather than after, is
  cheaper than fixing eight copies later.
