METHOD DISCOVERIES
--------------------
- A checkpoint-and-standards system that itself takes real time to
  maintain is not exempt from the same question it exists to answer for
  everything else: is this actually serving the objective, or has it
  become the objective? This project's own persistence discipline
  survived that question (kept, because it's genuinely what let this
  pivot get recorded cleanly); the freeze/phase-gating did not.
- When a user asks "can you see this from my perspective," the honest
  move is to check the record for whether their framing is actually
  true, not to agree performatively or to hedge defensively. Here it
  checked out exactly as stated — that's what made simply confirming it
  the right response instead of either capitulating or arguing.
- Verifying a person's stated credentials/track record against a primary
  source they provide (here, an actual resume) is the same
  `Standards/source-of-truth.md` discipline this project already applies
  to architecture claims — it applies to claims about the collaborators
  too, not just the artifact.
