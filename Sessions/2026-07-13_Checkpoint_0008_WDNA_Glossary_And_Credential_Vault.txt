---------------------

1. Context: Bill's MCSE background. Bill explained he studied for MCSE
   certification years ago but it became obsolete (Agile's rise) before
   he finished the final exam — meaning Windows DNA and similar
   Microsoft enterprise-architecture patterns are genuinely familiar
   territory he's trying to reconnect with, not a new topic being
   introduced cold. Relevant framing for future sessions: WDNA-style
   three-tier thinking is likely to be Bill's most comfortable entry
   point into this project's more abstract architecture work, not an
   unfamiliar detour.

2. Standards/glossary.md — new standard, first entry WDNA. Bill asked
   for "Windows DNA as WDNA unless there is a more appropriate
   abbreviation... with WDNA as the source of truth for our
   terminology." Rather than accept the abbreviation and the
   three-tier definition on Bill's word alone (per Checkpoint 0007's
   source-of-truth rule, adopted the previous evening), ran a live
   WebSearch to verify. Result, cited in the glossary entry: the
   three-tier model (Presentation/Business/Data Services) is real,
   well-documented, including in Microsoft's own 1997 announcement
   (news.microsoft.com) and Wikipedia — classified `authoritative`.
   The abbreviation "WDNA" itself is NOT something Microsoft used
   historically (their own materials just say "DNA" or "Windows DNA" in
   full) — classified explicitly as this project's own disambiguating
   convention, not an external standard, since bare "DNA" would collide
   with the biological term. Both facts recorded with their distinct
   basis, in the same entry, rather than blurring them into one
   undifferentiated claim.

3. Bill's contributed document preserved and extracted. Bill's document
   ("Windows DNA Architecture Context and Placement of the Credential
   Indirection and Secure Secret Management Service") describes a new
   security component — a Credential Vault Service using credential
   indirection (opaque CRIDs, never raw credentials, mediated
   authentication, KMS-managed encryption, per-CRID access control,
   audit logging) — and places it in WDNA's Business Services tier
   (Security Services category), with its encrypted store in the Data
   Services tier. Preserved verbatim in a new file,
   Architecture/rosetta-stone-AI-Architecture/contributed-windows-dna-credential-vault-context.md,
   then extracted into two tracked, ID-tagged artifacts per this
   project's own standards rather than left as an unprocessed document:
   - SCM_RSAI_0001 (logical-architecture.md, System Component Models)
     — the WDNA tier placement itself.
   - SEC_RSAI_0001 (system-policies.md, Security Model) — the Vault's
     internal responsibilities (encryption, KMS, access control, audit).
   Linked via `partOf`/`dependsOn` per Standards/relationship-vocabulary.md.
   Both carry `basis: primary-source` with direct quotes from Bill's
   document as the supporting evidence, consistent with
   Standards/source-of-truth.md. Both are new, additive architecture
   content — not a re-derivation of any existing `rosetta-stone-AI`
   node, and don't depend on Phase 1 training completing first.

METHOD DISCOVERIES
--------------------
- The source-of-truth rule (Checkpoint 0007) is easiest to apply
  correctly right at the moment new content is being authored, not as a
  retrofit — this session's glossary entry and the two SCM/SEC artifacts
  all got their `basis` classification live, during creation, rather
  than added after the fact. Verifying Bill's WDNA claim via WebSearch
  took one extra tool call and caught a real distinction (the model is
  authoritative; the abbreviation isn't) that would have been lost if
  the document had just been trusted and transcribed as given.
- A contributed source document doesn't have to choose between "kept
  verbatim" and "integrated into the standards system" — doing both
  (a preserved-as-given file plus extracted ID-tagged artifacts that
  cite it as their primary source) keeps the original contribution
  intact while still making its content trackable, cross-referenceable,
  and consistent with everything else in Architecture/.
