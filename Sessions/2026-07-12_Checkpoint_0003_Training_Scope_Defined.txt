WORKING AGREEMENT
------------------
Extends the Checkpoint 0002 agreement with an explicit scope boundary:
every training conversation must stay strictly within the nodes of the
frozen `rosetta-stone-AI` map — all fields (Responsibilities,
Collaborators, Inputs/Outputs, Metrics & Thresholds, Known Failure
Modes, Runtime Hooks, Regulatory Notes, Owner, Acceptance Criteria)
across all branches, and nothing outside that. `rosetta-stone-MCP-Bridge`
and any tangential AI-product/vendor discussion are explicitly out of
scope. Full detail in `Topics/bduf-freeze-and-training.md`.

OBJECTIVES COMPLETED
---------------------

1. Bill proposed an "AI Car Conceptual Model (Responsibility-Driven
   Design)" as a scope-check analogy — an implementation-agnostic
   Responsibility/Collaboration model for a car (Engine, Transmission,
   Axle, Tires, Body, Steering, AC, Alternator), explicitly excluding
   engine type, transmission type, and product features as irrelevant
   to a component's core Responsibility. The framing question: does
   this correctly convey what he wants the training to look like?

2. Confirmed the analogy is a correct instance of the RDD principle
   already embedded in the frozen map itself — specifically, the map's
   "Deployment Context Notes" node already treats Model's
   API-service/Edge/RAG variants the same way the car analogy treats
   engine types: deployment/technology variants that don't redefine the
   core Responsibility. Also flagged one open question before treating
   the analogy as the literal training frame: the car model used only
   Responsibilities + Collaborations, a narrower field set than the 9
   fields every node in `rosetta-stone-AI` actually carries, and asked
   whether that narrowing was intentional.

3. Bill resolved the question: he wants training scoped to **everything
   or only** the nodes of the `rosetta-stone-AI` diagram — all fields,
   not the reduced Responsibilities/Collaborations-only slice the car
   analogy used — specifically because he doesn't yet know what he
   doesn't know, so pre-narrowing the field set risks leaving
   comprehension gaps. The car analogy's actual purpose was validating
   shared understanding of the RDD frame, not defining a reduced
   training curriculum. He also stated the reasoning for the scope
   discipline itself: full node-by-node comprehension is what lets him
   engage with Claude's future questions intelligently, which is the
   precondition for the common foundational understanding the later
   investigate/tear-down/rebuild phase depends on — so conversations
   drifting outside the map's nodes would undermine that premise.

4. Updated `Topics/bduf-freeze-and-training.md` with a new "Training
   scope" section capturing this decision precisely: what's in scope
   (every field, every node, every branch including Model's 3
   deployment variants), what's explicitly out of scope
   (`rosetta-stone-MCP-Bridge`, tangential vendor/product discussion),
   and the rationale. Left the training's actual mechanics (format,
   cadence, whether Claude quizzes) as an open question — scope and
   format are separate decisions, and only scope was resolved this
   checkpoint.
