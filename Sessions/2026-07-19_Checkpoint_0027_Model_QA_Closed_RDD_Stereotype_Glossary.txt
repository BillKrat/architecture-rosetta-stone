OBJECTIVES COMPLETED

1. Closed Model's Q&A (per Bill directly): no pseudocode warranted for
   Retrieval at Model's altitude — a plain-language explanation was
   enough (Retrieval finds/ranks grounding content; Agent, not Model,
   actually calls Retrieval and assembles results into Model's context
   window). Retrieval-as-a-Model-collaborator stays exactly as flagged
   in the pristine doc: a candidate, not settled. Bill's own hypothesis

   collaborator once the remaining nodes get their own Q&A passes —
   left as a hypothesis to test later, not resolved by editing the
   pristine doc now.

2. Bill raised a mental-model question: he defaults to reading
   "Controller" as ASP.NET MVC's `Controller` class (a pattern he
   considers dated since "smart controls" absorbed its decision logic),
   drawing a parallel to early confusion in his clinical-trial
   development career over "Developer" terminology. Explained that
   RDD's six Object Role Stereotypes are a general responsibility
   classification, independent of any framework or era — MVC's
   Controller was one historical implementation of the stereotype, not
   its definition; the "smart controls" shift is the *responsibility*
   migrating to a new owner, not the *stereotype* going obsolete.

3. Added a "RDD Stereotypes at a Glance" section to the pristine
   reference doc, placed right before the 9 nodes, per Bill's explicit
   request — a one-line gloss per stereotype, which node(s) carry each,
   RDD's own anti-overfitting caution quote (p.93), and an explicit flag
   that the one-line glosses are this project's own working
   characterization, not verbatim book text (only the name list and the
   caution quote are direct-quote-grounded so far).

4. Corrected a stale claim in Standards/glossary.md's RDD entry — it
   read as though Ch.3 pp.93-94 (stereotype elaboration) was still
   unscanned as of 2026-07-14, but the pristine doc's own "Method and
   grounding" section already showed it was directly re-confirmed on
   2026-07-17 (Checkpoint 0017) alongside Ch.4-5. This was Claude's own
   initially-wrong claim to Bill in this same session (repeating the
   stale glossary line without cross-checking the pristine doc first);
   caught and corrected within the same turn once the discrepancy
   surfaced. Updated the glossary entry's stereotype bullet and its
   Status line to match reality, and pointed it at the new in-doc
   glossary.

5. Bill caught a real wording bug during Agent's own Q&A: "Tool —
   invokes Tools for work the Model can't do reliably itself" implied
   Model *can* do these things (run code, query a database, fetch live
   data), just unreliably — false. Model has no execution environment
   or live network/database access at all; it's a hard incapacity, not
   a reliability gap, consistent with the Model Q&A's own established
   finding that Model has no access to anything outside its trained
   weights at inference time. Fixed both occurrences (Agent's Tool
   collaborator line, Tool's own Responsibilities bullet) to "can't do
   itself" / "cannot do itself." Logged in the QA log with the reasoning
   and one flagged exception: a genuine reliability-only case (e.g.
   precise large-number arithmetic) would still warrant "reliably" if
   ever added as a Tool example — not true of anything currently listed.
