     asks questions, Bill works through them, and when Claude judges

   - Phase 2: once every checkbox from Phase 1 is checked, uncheck them

     agree on each node's actual correctness (not just comprehension)
     — nodes may be added, expanded, or contracted as warranted. Sign
     off (check the box again) as each node is mutually agreed correct.
   - Phase 3: repeat Phases 1-2 on `rosetta-stone-MCP-Bridge` (the
     parked Concern B document) once Concern A is fully signed off.
   - Phase 4: gap analysis — current state (the two finalized maps)
     versus target state (what's actually needed).
   - Phase 5: requirements document and backlog — the handoff into
     actual development.
   Bill's closing framing: "That is the roadmap I envision, I look
   forward to your reasoning and training to get us successfully over
   the finish line" — i.e. explicit request for Claude's assessment of
   the plan before starting, then to begin acting as mentor/trainer
   through it.

3. Claude's review, given before persisting: the phase structure is
   sound because it keeps "do I understand this" (Phase 1) strictly
   separate from "is this actually correct" (Phase 2) — collapsing
   those two questions is a common way BDUF efforts drift, and this
   roadmap avoids it by gating each phase on explicit sign-off of the
   previous one. Flagged and confirmed with Bill that checkbox-toggling
   is compatible with the existing content freeze (it's status
   metadata, not the frozen Responsibility/Collaborator content itself).
