WORKING AGREEMENT
-----------------
Bill set two new working patterns this session, both durable:

1. **One question at a time.** Q&A on the node-definitions content
   proceeds one exchange per turn, not batched, so nothing scrolls out
   of view before it's discussed. Applies to all future node Q&A, not
   just Model.
2. **Hold polish edits until a node's Q&A has gone far enough to state
   the answer clearly, rather than editing after every exchange.** Bill's
   own words: the edits "may evolve more as you start to recognize the
   ignorance and take me from it to understanding — then you can clearly
   state it for the next ignorant developer." Raw findings get logged
   (see the new "Q&A log" subsection pattern in the topic file); the
   polished Responsibility/Collaborator bullets only get rewritten once
   a node's Q&A is far enough along. This is now the standard pattern
   for future node Q&A sessions, not a one-off for Model.

OBJECTIVES COMPLETED
---------------------

1. Walked Bill through a concrete RAG example (upload a research
   document, then query it) to ground Model's actual boundary. Found
   and corrected his initial assumption that the document itself gets
   sent to the Model — it doesn't, at any point. Traced the real
   sequence: text extraction -> chunking -> embedding (a separate,
   smaller model, not the same generative Model) -> vector storage ->
   query embedding -> similarity search (pure math against a
   precomputed index, no large model involved) -> chunk retrieval as
   text -> context assembly -> only then does the Model generate.

2. Distinguished the similarity-search step from the Model's generation
   step precisely, since Bill flagged they can blur together: search
   compares two vectors against a stored index (fixed math, no learned
   judgment in the moment); generation runs the assembled context
   through the Model's trained weights to synthesize new text. Neither
   one touches training data directly.

3. Corrected a natural but wrong mental model Bill proposed (that
   generation "works off of training data" the way search works off
   context): the Model has no access to its training data at inference
   time at all. Training data shaped the weights once, during training,
   and isn't present or consultable afterward — the Model runs a fixed
   function over whatever's currently in its context window, nothing
   else.

4. Resolved who owns context-window assembly, which Bill correctly
   guessed wasn't Model's job but initially attributed to Memory.
   Corrected: Memory is a container (information holder per its RDD
   stereotype) that supplies raw material on request; deciding what to
   include, how much, and in what order is a decision, which belongs to
   Agent. This surfaced a real, previously-unnoticed gap — Agent's
   Responsibilities as written in Checkpoint 0017 never actually named
   context assembly — fixed directly in
   `Topics/rosetta-stone-ai-node-definitions.md` rather than left as a
   TODO.

5. Bill explicitly asked to capture this Q&A as a blog post (his
   framing: "solid gold... at least at my phase of knowledge"), and to
   batch it by node rather than fragment per exchange — a new post was
   written in `vs-mcp-bridge` capturing the whole Model walkthrough as a
   standalone teaching piece, following the established
   `save-for-future-blog-content` pattern from Checkpoint 0016.
